Smart City IoT

ADSPL provides IoT solutions for smart cities and connected technology helps promote economic development, improve infrastructure & environment, enhance transportation systems and optimize costs of managing public assets. Cloud-based IoT applications receive, analyze, and manage data in real-time to help municipalities, enterprises, and citizens make better decisions that improve quality of life.

Smart Buildings & Properties

  1. Safety & Security Systems:
    Implementing remote monitoring, biometrics, IP surveillance cameras, and wireless alarms to reduce unauthorized access to buildings and chances of thefts.
  2. Smart Garden & Sprinkler System:
    Smart sprinkler system synced with connected technologies and cloud can be used to water plants with the assurance that plants get the right amount of water.
  3. Smart Heating & Ventilation:
    Smart heating and ventilation systems monitor various parameters such as temperature, pressure, vibration, humidity of the buildings and properties such as movie theatres, and historical monuments.

Smart Industrial Environment

  1. Forest Fire Detection:
    Monitoring of combustion gases and preemptive fire conditions to define alert zones.
  2. Air/Noise Pollution:
    Control of CO2 emissions of factories, pollution emitted by cars and toxic gases generated on farms
  3. Snow Level Monitoring:
    Identification of the real-time condition of ski tracks, allowing security corporations for avalanche prevention.
  4. Landslide and Avalanche Avoidance:
    Monitoring of soil moisture, earth density, as well as vibrations to identify dangerous patterns in land conditions.
  5. Earthquake Early Detection:
    Detecting the chances of tremors by utilizing distributed controls at specific places of tremors.
  6. Liquid Presence:
    Detecting the presence of liquid in data centers, building grounds, and warehouses to prevent breakdowns and corrosion.
  7. Radiation Levels:
    Distributed measurement of radiation levels in nuclear power stations surroundings to generate leakage alerts.
  8. Explosive and Hazardous Gases:
    Detecting gas levels and leakages in chemical factories, industrial environments, and inside mines.

  1. Smart Grid:
    Smart grids are digitally monitored, self-healing energy systems that deliver electricity or gas from generation sources.

    Monitors the quality of tap water in the cities.

    Identifies leakages and wastes of factories in rivers.

Smart Waste Management
