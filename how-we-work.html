1. ADSPL Work Culture

We Work Remotely?

Company target was to become remote-first Company since day one, in order to attract and retain some of the most talented designers, developers, data scientists and project managers from across the country. We wanted to only hire the best and highly motivated individuals with excellent communication skills. With our remote setup we’re able to give clients a seamless working experience, even though we’re not onsite.

Communication

We value client engagement as highly collaborative partnership. We have set up our business model in a way that we can deliver the product in a very efficient way to our customers and it’s crucial to have consistent follow up with our clients to make sure we’re always working on the right things. We use all communication channels to have interactions with our clients to allow them feel just as involved in the development process as they would if we were on-site.

Team Working Conduct

To ensure that our work style is as effective as working together in person, we have worked out a working conduct for our team to ensure we are focussed and connected to our priorities and can deliver the projects on schedule.

Each team member is responsible for having a distraction-free workspace, whether they choose a co-working space or have a home office.

To make the video conferencing experience as seamless as possible, all team members must have access to at least a consistent 5Mbps connection.

Team members must either use a headset with an inline or attached microphone or a podcast-quality desktop mic.

Our core working hours are from 9 a.m. – 6 p.m. IST. We work with clients for 45 high-quality hours/week, reserving the remaining 5 hours for professional development and internal projects.

ADSPL team will be available to our customers for active collaboration during regular business hours. We’ve tested many different collaboration tools, and we only use the best of the best. Here are the five must-have tools we use to collaborate with each other and with our clients.

  • Communication: We use mail communication, what’s the app and other possible communication modes to reach our client. We also integrate with third-party tools, like Pivotal and Google Drive.
  • Video Conferencing: We use Skype/Google Duo/Whats app/Zoom to do voice/video calls. Depending upon the requirement individual or small groups or large teams concurrently, these tools provide a quick and easy way for us to communicate with clients face to face and is by far the most reliable video call services.
  • Project Management: We do proper planning and scheduling and use MS Project or MS excel for our development workflow. Stories can have task lists, attachments, and conversation threads. They are grouped and organized to fit the evolution of your product.
  • Document Management: Google Drive is our primary repository for all project-related documentation, from contracts and proposals to design files. You’ve been shared on the root project folder and can collaborate in real-time on any documents in your folder.
  • Code Repository: We will store one of your most important assets — your application’s code — on Github. Its version control tooling allows multiple developers to work on your application at the same time without overriding each other’s work.

Incase our client or customer wants to kick-off any project, we have team members reach out for an in-person to discuss the Project Scope. We put together a tight agenda in advance and typically work a couple of full days together. Our product managers are also available to come onsite for additional Product Strategy discussion on an as-needed basis
